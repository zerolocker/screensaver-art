Choose what plays — selection UX prototypes

Live, clickable prototypes using the real gallery (182 pieces, previews streamed from R2). The default everywhere is the first 100 pieces.

The candidate

Option I — current

Filter header explorations

Three treatments of the All/Selected + styles header (tabs · one row · integrated into the page header), flippable live via the corner switcher. No labels, no "Playing" chip, counts on the scope control.

 Option H

E's poster engine + F's two-row filters

The round-2 pick: poster-frame thumbnails, Show/Styles rows with SMALL-CAPS labels. Superseded by Option I's header treatments.

Round 2 — Option A + your feedback

All three: instant apply (no Save), tick toggles / card-click opens a detail modal (title, style, date), style filter pills, a Selected view, no search/size/reset. They differ in layout and in how they keep video rendering cheap.

Option E

Style pills + instant ticks

One pill row: All · Selected · nine art styles. Thumbnails are captured first frames (no live videos at rest); hover briefly to see motion.

  • Strengths: simplest mental model; lightest scrolling — zero live videos until you hover
  • Watch out: grid is static at rest (no ambient motion)
 Option F

Virtualized grid

Keeps live hover-to-play video cards, but only ~viewport's worth exist in the DOM at a time. Multi-select style pills + All/Selected switch.

  • Strengths: scales to thousands of pieces with live video; pills combine ("Asia + Medieval")
  • Watch out: most engineering; combining filters is a heavier mental model
 Option G

Style rows + bulk select

One horizontal row per style with "Add all / Remove all" — curate by taste in a few clicks, fine-tune with ticks. Pills jump to a style.

  • Strengths: fastest path to a taste-based selection; styles become a browsing structure, not just a filter
  • Watch out: horizontal scrolling hides most of each style; needs good row ordering

Round 1 — first explorations

Option A

Select in the gallery

The existing gallery grid becomes the picker — checkmarks on every card, like selecting photos in Apple Photos.

  • Strengths: familiar, direct, almost no new UI to learn
  • Watch out: hand-picking 182+ pieces gets tedious, and a manual selection goes stale as new art lands daily
 Option B

Smart mixes

Pick a strategy, not pieces: Classic 100, Latest 100, a random mix, everything — or fully hand-picked. Exclude individual pieces from any mix.

  • Strengths: fits a gallery that grows daily — new art joins automatically; one tap to a great setup
  • Watch out: less direct control; the mix concept needs clear copy
 Option C

Playlist builder

A music-app layout: library on the left, your ordered playlist on the right, drag to reorder.

  • Strengths: most control — including play order; the playlist is an explicit, inspectable thing
  • Watch out: heaviest UI; play order may not matter much for a screensaver
 Option D

Tune as you browse

No upfront curation: Featured 100 plays by default. Hide pieces you dislike, heart the ones you love, switch to favorites-only anytime.

  • Strengths: zero effort to start; matches how people actually behave (react, don't curate)
  • Watch out: "what plays" is implicit; favorites-only is empty until you've hearted things

Notes: selections persist per-prototype in localStorage (each page has a reset), the cache-size figure assumes ~12 MB per clip, and videos lazy-load as you scroll — the first paint of a card is the clip's first frame, hover to play. None of this is wired to the real app yet; it's purely for comparing interaction models.
